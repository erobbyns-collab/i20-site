High Street Bank Deserts

More than six thousand bank branches have closed across the United Kingdom since 2015, leaving hundreds of communities without any physical banking presence at all. The burden falls hardest on older residents, small traders and the digitally excluded, for whom a branch is not nostalgia but infrastructure. The Cash Acceptance Cliff

Roughly one in twenty adults in Britain still relies on cash for most daily spending, yet acceptance is eroding café by café, car park by car park. When notes and coins stop working, budgeting tools used by low-income households quietly stop working too. The Regional Productivity Divide

Britain's productivity puzzle is really a geography puzzle: London and the South East generate output per hour that much of the rest of the country cannot match, and the difference compounds into pay, tax receipts and local investment.
